Lesson 3: Weather and Climate

Bilingual Guide for ESL Students
Weather is the day-to-day change in atmospheric conditions at a particular place. Climate is the average long-term weather conditions in a region. All weather and climate ultimately arise from the uneven heating of the Earth by the sun's rays [cite: 186-187, 207-208].
Figure 1: Global Wind Patterns
This uneven heating causes global wind circulation patterns and worldwide ocean currents. Warm air at the equator rises and moves toward the poles, while cold air at the poles sinks and moves toward the equator. Ocean currents also help transfer heat from the equatorial regions to the poles [cite: 209-211, 214-216].
Daily weather is caused by the movement of Air Masses. The boundary between two air masses is called a Front. Meteorologists study weather patterns and use satellites to predict storms and issue warnings [cite: 217-219, 223-227].

Lesson 4: Earth in the Solar System

The solar system consists of the sun, the planets, their moons, asteroids, and comets. The sun contains more than 99 percent of the mass in the solar system [cite: 269-274].
There are eight planets. Inner Planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) are small and rocky. Outer Planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune) are gas giants located far from the sun [cite: 275-278].
