Lesson 2: Earth's Resources

[cite_start]A resource is anything humans need to survive[cite: 86]. [cite_start]Resources are either renewable (endless supply) or nonrenewable (limited supply)[cite: 83, 84].
Nonrenewable Resources take millions of years to form naturally. [cite_start]When used up, they cannot be replaced[cite: 93, 94]. [cite_start]Examples include soil, minerals, and Fossil Fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas[cite: 94, 95, 97].
Renewable Resources cycle through the environment or exist in unlimited supply. [cite_start]Examples include solar energy, water, wind, and Geothermal power from Earth's crust[cite: 98, 99].
[cite_start]Conservation: Humans harm resources through overconsumption and pollution[cite: 101]. [cite_start]Conservation limits overuse through recycling, building efficient engines, and developing renewable technologies[cite: 106, 107, 108].
