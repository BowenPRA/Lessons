What is a Pronoun? (Đại từ là gì?)
A pronoun is a short word that takes the place of a noun (a person, place, or thing).
  • Reason we use them: We use pronouns so we do not have to repeat the same noun over and over.
  • Example: Instead of saying, "Maria left Maria's phone on Maria's desk," we say, "Maria left her phone on her desk."

How Pronouns Appear on the GED Test

On the GED RLA test, you will be asked to fix sentences where pronouns are used incorrectly. The test focuses on these main rules:
Rule 1: Pronoun Agreement (Sự hòa hợp số ít/số nhiều)
A pronoun must match the noun it replaces. If the noun is singular (one), the pronoun must be singular. If the noun is plural (many), the pronoun must be plural.
  • ☒ Incorrect: The company requires their employees to wear uniforms. (Company is ONE thing, so "their" is wrong).
  • ☑ Correct: The company requires its employees to wear uniforms.
Rule 2: Unclear Pronouns (Đại từ không rõ ràng)
If a sentence has two people or two things of the same gender/type, a pronoun can confuse the reader. You must rewrite the sentence to make it completely clear.
  • ☒ Incorrect: When John spoke to David, he was angry. (Who was angry? John or David? It is unclear!)
  • ☑ Correct: When John spoke to David, John was angry.
Rule 3: The "They" vs. "He or She" Trap
This is a very common trick on the GED test! In everyday spoken English, people often use the plural pronouns they, them, or their to talk about one person if they don't know the person's gender (like "a student," "a customer," or "anybody").
However, formal writing on the GED test requires strict grammar. On the GED, they/them/their is STRICTLY PLURAL (more than one). Because words like "a customer" or "a person" are singular, you MUST use the singular pronoun he or she (or his or her).
  • ☒ Incorrect (Everyday): If a customer calls, tell them to wait.
  • ☑ Correct (GED): If a customer calls, tell him or her to wait.
Teacher's Tip: If "him or her" sounds awkward, the best way to fix it is to make the noun plural: If customers call, tell them to wait.
