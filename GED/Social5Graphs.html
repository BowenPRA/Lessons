Bilingual Study Guide & Vocabulary
Key Ideas
  • Tables and graphs are commonly used to present data in social studies materials.
  • A Measure of Central Tendency is used to summarize data with one number (like the Mean or Median).
  • Bar graphs are used to compare data, and line graphs are used to show Trends.
Understanding social studies materials requires basic calculations. You will do math on the GED Social Studies Test, including finding the Arithmetic Mean (the average) or the Median (the exact middle value of an ordered list).

Example 1: Calculating Averages

Franklin County Family Finances (Family of 4)
Year Median Income Average % to Taxes Average % to Savings
2009$40,80027%5.2%
2010$41,50028%6.0%
2011$42,30029%5.5%
2012$44,10029%5.1%
Question: What is the average percentage of income that a family saved each year between 2009 and 2012?

How to solve: To find the average (mean), add up the figures from the "Average % to Savings" column and divide by the number of years (4).
5.2 + 6.0 + 5.5 + 5.1 = 21.8
21.8 ÷ 4 = 5.45%

Example 2: Reading Chart Trends

Graphs help us visualize comparisons. For example, a grouped bar chart allows you to quickly compare the growth rate of two different categories (like Overseas arrivals vs. North American arrivals) side-by-side across multiple years.
