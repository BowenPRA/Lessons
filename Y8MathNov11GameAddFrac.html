How to Add Fractions

Step 0: Convert Mixed Numbers (Hard Mode)

If you have mixed numbers, convert them to improper fractions first.

Example: 2 1/3 = (2 √ó 3 + 1)/3 = 7/3

Step 1: Find the LCD (Lowest Common Denominator)

Find the smallest number that both denominators can divide into evenly.

Example: For 1/4 + 1/6
Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16...
Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18...
LCD = 12

Step 2: Rewrite Each Fraction

Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the LCD as the denominator.

1/4 = ?/12 ‚Üí Multiply top and bottom by 3 ‚Üí 3/12
1/6 = ?/12 ‚Üí Multiply top and bottom by 2 ‚Üí 2/12

Step 3: Add the Numerators

Add the numerators together, keeping the same denominator.

3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12

Step 4: Simplify (if needed)

Reduce the fraction to its simplest form by dividing both numerator and denominator by their GCD. Convert back to mixed number if improper.

6/12 = 1/2 (divide both by 6)
7/3 = 2 1/3 (improper to mixed)
